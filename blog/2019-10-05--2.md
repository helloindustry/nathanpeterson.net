<h1>our strength</h1><p>When we wake up in the morning and Fear already has a hold around our neck, we can choose to take a step to the side of the problems filling our view. Then we can ask a question like, "what do I need right now?" We can choose to be gentle and caring with ourselves. We can take 30 minutes to exercise, or stretch, or drink a coffee and enjoy the taste. We can choose to *breathe*. This is the breath Fear was trying to take from us when we awoke.</p><p>The problem moments ago wasn't the circumstances filling our view. The problem was our perspective. No need to feel bad about that &mdash; Fear just got up a little earlier than we did today. But now that we have our perspective back &mdash; now that we're breathing again &mdash; we can choose to see the whole picture. And while Fear hasn't gone anywhere, and while our circumstances haven't changed, our strength is there, and it is sufficient.</p><p>We are stronger than we realize. We just need to know where our strength comes from.</p>
