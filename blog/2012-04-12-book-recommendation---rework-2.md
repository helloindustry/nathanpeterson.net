<p align="center"><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Rework-Jason-Fried/dp/0307463745" target="_blank"><img alt="Rework by Jason Fried and David Heinemeier Hansson" src="/images/6e2729f05707dda8118bb1a5c62d7d0882d76a79bd89f32e6e4a7b61a1a6eb20.jpg" /></a></p>&#13;
<p>This is the most helpful business book I've read, ever. That's a pretty big statement considering what a business-book junkie I am. I just finished reading it for a second time.</p>&#13;
<p><strong>I <em>highly</em> recommend this book to anyone willing to re-think the way they approach their work.</strong></p>&#13;
<p>Here are a few of my (many) highlights from the book:</p>&#13;
<blockquote>&#13;
<p><em>"Writing a plan makes you feel in control of things you can't actually control."</em></p>&#13;
<p><em>"Workaholics aren't heroes. They don't save the day, they just use it up. The real hero is already home because she figured out a faster way to get things done."</em></p>&#13;
<p><em>"If no one's upset by what you're saying, you're probably not pushing hard enough . (And you're probably boring too.)"</em></p>&#13;
<p><em>"Policies are organizational scar tissue. They are codified overreactions to situations that are unlikely to happen again."</em></p>&#13;
<p><em>"Momentum fuels motivation."</em></p>&#13;
<p><em>"The way you build momentum is by getting something done and then moving on to the next thing."</em></p>&#13;
</blockquote>
