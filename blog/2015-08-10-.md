<p>People keep thanking us for sharing our journey. They keep telling us how strong we are. I&rsquo;m thankful for the encouragements and I do agree that God has made us strong for this. BUT, I think it&rsquo;s easy to overlook the crowd of friends and family and even many strangers who have surrounded us. So I have a couple thoughts:</p>

<p>1. We&rsquo;re strong at times and weak other times. But as a community surrounding Olivia and the idea she brings (that beauty is more valuable than efficiency) we are very strong. The strength you&rsquo;re seeing in us is likely the strength of our community, which you&rsquo;re likely a part of.</p>

<p>2. This isn&rsquo;t just our story. Olivia isn&rsquo;t just ours. I feel like she is and if you try to touch her I&rsquo;ll probably attack you :), but deep down we know she belongs to God and her story is all of our story and her life is for all of us. Furthermore, I believe the same is true for all of our lives. I don&rsquo;t own my children. I don&rsquo;t own my self. I&rsquo;m responsible, but not in possession. There&rsquo;s a big difference, especially when it comes to how we view sharing our lives with each other.</p>
