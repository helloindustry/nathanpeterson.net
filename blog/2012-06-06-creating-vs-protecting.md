<p>There’s a constant pull in me, between my desire to create and my need to protect myself. <strong>To create is to give</strong>, yet I tend to favor protection.</p>&#13;
<p>To create, for me, looks like:</p>&#13;
<ul><li>writing music and lyrics</li>&#13;
<li>recording</li>&#13;
<li>performing</li>&#13;
<li>releasing songs and ideas</li>&#13;
<li>thinking, reading, exercise, traveling, creative intake</li>&#13;
<li>praying</li>&#13;
<li>inquiring</li>&#13;
<li>quietness</li>&#13;
<li>waiting</li>&#13;
</ul><p><br />To protect looks like:</p>&#13;
<ul><li>over-producing</li>&#13;
<li>activity, busyness</li>&#13;
<li>networking, campaigning</li>&#13;
<li>meetings</li>&#13;
<li>gear research</li>&#13;
<li>advertising, marketing</li>&#13;
<li>maintaining strategic relationships</li>&#13;
<li>manipulating</li>&#13;
<li>making myself heard, shouting</li>&#13;
<li>tweeting to make myself seem interesting or important</li>&#13;
<li>strategizing</li>&#13;
<li>endless re-evaluating</li>&#13;
<li>policy creating, mistake avoidance</li>&#13;
</ul><p><br />There are times when things on the 2nd list are necessary, but they should never be prioritized over things on the 1st list. Unfortunately, I find that to be the case <em>most</em> of the time. <strong>I protect myself.</strong> And only when I feel totally safe (or, alternatively, completely broken) do I choose to create.</p>&#13;
<p>Instead of giving, I end up taking. Instead of living, I maintain. Creative tasks are moved to the bottom of the to-do list. Not because they’re less important (they’re <em>most</em> important), but because they’re scary. I feel threatened, so I can’t create. I can’t give.</p>&#13;
<p>Is this really living?</p>&#13;
<p>What would it look like to live a life of <strong>creativity without concern for security</strong>? What would I do differently today? And if I made this change, <em>do I really think I’d be any less secure?</em></p>&#13;
<p><strong>Protection, at best, delays death</strong> (doesn’t avoid it). It maintains. Nothing more. Nothing positive. Net zero.</p>&#13;
<p><strong>Generous creativity changes everything.</strong> It moves us forward. It feeds our souls. It’s exciting! And yes, it’s scary as hell. But very few times is the <em>best</em> decision also the <em>safest</em> one.</p>
