<p>My favorite coffees ever have been as a result of a Chemex brew. But my most frequent and consistent very <em>very</em> good coffees have come from the Clever Dripper brewer. There is by far no easier and more consistent way to brew a great cup of coffee at home, at least that I'm aware of.</p>&#13;
<p><strong>Here's how I use it:</strong></p>&#13;
<ol><li>Grind 20g of fresh coffee grounds, fairly fine. Like you'd do for your automatic dripper.</li>&#13;
<li>Boil water.</li>&#13;
<li>Place filter in brewer.</li>&#13;
<li>Fill brewer with boiling water, then place on cup to let water release (this rinses out the paper filter and pre-heats the brewer).</li>&#13;
<li>Pour 20g of ground coffee into brewer.</li>&#13;
<li>Set timer for 4min.</li>&#13;
<li>Pour 350g of water (just after it's finished boiling) onto grounds.</li>&#13;
<li>Start timer.</li>&#13;
<li>Stir around and pat down the bloom until everything is mixed together well.</li>&#13;
<li>Scrape any grounds stuck to the side of the filter down and stir into the water.</li>&#13;
<li>Cover top until about 1:30. Then stir around a few times.</li>&#13;
<li>Cover top again until the timer goes off.</li>&#13;
<li>Stir one last time and then place brewer on a cup (make sure it's big enough to hold 350g!).</li>&#13;
<li>Should take about 1:30 to release all of the coffee. If it goes way faster, use a finer grind next time. If it goes way slower or never finishes emptying, use a courser grind next time.</li>&#13;
<li>Clean up and then enjoy your coffee!</li>&#13;
</ol><p><strong><br /></strong></p>&#13;
<p><strong>Other tools I need to make my coffee this way are:</strong></p>&#13;
<ul><li>Decent burr grinder</li>&#13;
<li>Kitchen scale (digital is easier)</li>&#13;
<li>Electric kettle for boiling water (you could use a stovetop kettle though)</li>&#13;
<li>Decent coffee</li>&#13;
<li>A spoon for stirring</li>&#13;
<li>Cup big enough to catch 350g (about 12oz) of coffee</li>&#13;
</ul>
