<p>I used to picture grief as a necessary step in the process&mdash;something to be &ldquo;gotten through&rdquo; in order for healing to come. Now I realize that there is no separating grief from healing. Grief is the process by which we are healed. Grief is healing.</p><p><br></p><p>This shift is so important because it moves the pain and suffering and insanity of grief from a thing to be tolerated or &ldquo;gotten through&rdquo;, to a thing to be accepted and even cherished. Like in childbirth, the pain is good because it is a signal that new life is coming.</p><p><br></p><p>I now see grief as clarity. It is a lack of layers which we are usually comforted by. With those layers folded back, the discomfort is unbearable. But a wound can&rsquo;t be healed if there is something in the way. It needs to be open in order to be healed. Grief is the feeling of being open&mdash;the necessary position for healing.</p>
