---
date: 2012-06-06
title: "Creating vs Protecting"
---

There’s a constant pull in me, between my desire to create and my need to
protect myself. __To create is to give__ , yet I tend to favor protection.

To create, for me, looks like:

  * writing music and lyrics
  * recording
  * performing
  * releasing songs and ideas
  * thinking, reading, exercise, traveling, creative intake
  * praying
  * inquiring
  * quietness
  * waiting

  
To protect looks like:

  * over-producing
  * activity, busyness
  * networking, campaigning
  * meetings
  * gear research
  * advertising, marketing
  * maintaining strategic relationships
  * manipulating
  * making myself heard, shouting
  * tweeting to make myself seem interesting or important
  * strategizing
  * endless re-evaluating
  * policy creating, mistake avoidance

  
There are times when things on the 2nd list are necessary, but they should
never be prioritized over things on the 1st list. Unfortunately, I find that
to be the case *most* of the time. __I protect myself.__ And only when I feel
totally safe (or, alternatively, completely broken) do I choose to create.

Instead of giving, I end up taking. Instead of living, I maintain. Creative
tasks are moved to the bottom of the to-do list. Not because they’re less
important (they’re *most* important), but because they’re scary. I feel
threatened, so I can’t create. I can’t give.

Is this really living?

What would it look like to live a life of __creativity without concern for
security__? What would I do differently today? And if I made this change, *do
I really think I’d be any less secure?*

__Protection, at best, delays death__ (doesn’t avoid it). It maintains.
Nothing more. Nothing positive. Net zero.

__Generous creativity changes everything.__ It moves us forward. It feeds our
souls. It’s exciting! And yes, it’s scary as hell. But very few times is the
*best* decision also the *safest* one.
