---
date: 2012-12-01
title: "Avoiding Excitement"
---
<p>There&#8217;s something within me that blows off things that excite me in exchange for things more generally accepted by my culture. If I pursue what excites me I have to feel selfish and guilty. If I pursue accepted things, I feel righteous - like God is happy with me. Like giving up magic to become a missionary. It sounds like the right thing to do. But it doesn't feel right at all.</p>

<p>I wonder what would happen if, instead of avoiding things that excite us, we let whatever excites us, whatever is most alive in us, <em>drive</em> our decisions and our direction. Would we really end up so far from what God intended when He thought us up?</p>
<p>I also wonder if there might be a little part of us that just uses God as an excuse to avoid excitement and the fear and risk that come along with things worth being excited about.</p>
