title: "Blog Post: Ignore Outcomes"
---
<p>Things just kind of happen. Sometimes they&rsquo;re predictable, sometimes they&rsquo;re not. Sometimes they&rsquo;re positive, sometimes not. It all seems to balance out in the end. What&rsquo;s interesting to me is the seemingly and surprisingly little correlation between specific actions and outcomes. &ldquo;The rain falls on the righteous as well as the unrighteousness.&rdquo; My conclusion is that <strong>action for the sake of a particular outcome is a mistake.</strong> It's a recipe for disillusionment.</p>
<p>So why try?</p>
<p>We should try because <strong>trying is inherently good, regardless of outcome</strong>. Write a book to write a book. Write a song to write a song. Help someone to help someone. Start an enterprise to start an enterprise.</p>
<p>It's called <em>living</em>. Outcomes are irrelevant.</p>
<p><strong>Focus on outcomes only paralyzes us</strong>. It rules our actions while really it should be the other way around.&nbsp;<strong>We should do what&rsquo;s most alive in us, not what&rsquo;s most likely to &ldquo;work&rdquo; or be accepted.</strong></p>
<p>How would it change the way we work, live, and love, if we ignored outcomes?</p>
