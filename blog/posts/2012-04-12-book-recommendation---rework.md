This is the most helpful business book I've read, ever. That's a pretty big
statement considering what a business-book junkie I am. I just finished
reading it for a second time.

__I *highly* recommend this book to anyone willing to re-think the way they
approach their work.__

Here are a few of my (many) highlights from the book:

> *"Writing a plan makes you feel in control of things you can't actually
control."*

>

> *"Workaholics aren't heroes. They don't save the day, they just use it up.
The real hero is already home because she figured out a faster way to get
things done."*

>

> *"If no one's upset by what you're saying, you're probably not pushing hard
enough . (And you're probably boring too.)"*

>

> *"Policies are organizational scar tissue. They are codified overreactions
to situations that are unlikely to happen again."*

>

> *"Momentum fuels motivation."*

>

> *"The way you build momentum is by getting something done and then moving on
to the next thing."*
