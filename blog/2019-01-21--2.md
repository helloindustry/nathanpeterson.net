<p>I think when people hear &ldquo;moving inward&rdquo; they assume it means shutting out the outside world... I&rsquo;m learning that it might be the exact opposite. It may be that our *only* way of truly connecting with our world and with each other is to first come back to where we are:</p><p><br></p><p>To do some letting go. Some accepting of where I am and of who I am, what I&rsquo;m like, my faults, my strengths, my failures, my successes... ALL OF IT. To be with this complex person who I&rsquo;ve become and to appreciate him, faults and all. To trust that I am beautifully made. To trust that my life is on a path and that it is going to be okay, even when it isn&rsquo;t. To allow myself to breathe and to just *be* and to trust the controlling of things to God&mdash;to the Current...</p><p><br></p><p>And from *that* place, look up and receive the gaze or the words or the touch of a friend.</p><p><br></p><p>What a scary, vulnerable thing to do. Why? Because anything could happen at that point, and we wouldn&rsquo;t be braced for it&mdash;we would be unprotected. And that is exactly why it could be so amazingly beautiful&mdash;anything could happen.</p>
